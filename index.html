About Me

Hi there! I am a FullStack Web Developer with a focus on the MERN stack. I am also a graduate student, studying Data Science to increase my technical skills. I recently graduated from a University of Minnesota Web Development Bootcamp. This intensive three month program was my segue into the exciting world of computer science. I decided to go to grad school afterwards because I felt as though there was so much more that I wanted to learn about this field. I hope to combine software development work experience with my studies to make strides in both software engineering and data science.

I spend my free time honing skills introduced during the bootcamp, such as data structures and algorithms. The more I learn, the deeper I fall in love with tech, and cannot wait to acquire even more skills at my next role!

Why Me?

I am a responsive developer that is passionate about what she does. I am always willing to learn new things and believe that one can never know enough about a topic. If I do not know how to solve a problem, I will always do my best to exhaust my resources until I learn how. I love this field because there will always be new developments within it. I will never have to feel stagnant and can always continue to learn, even as a senior developer!

My fully remote bootcamp equipped me with the skilled needed for colloborative remote development. I can utilize git, liveshare via the Visual Studio Code Editor, and Zoom screen-sharing to effectively work with others.

Since my graduate program is geared towards people who work fulltime, my workload is not intense enough to keep me from performing well with job duties. I will have plenty of time to dedicate towards my next role to ensure optimal performance. I am fully committed to any task at hand, whether the role requires me to hone my current skills or acquire entirely new ones. My primary programming language is JavaScript, and I am also currently learning Python in school. I am comfortable with developing in either of these languages, but would also not mind adding in a third! I am also open to working as either a front end, back end, or full stack developer. The way I see it, any experience is valuable and thus worth my time. Despite my flexibility, my preference would be to work with the MERN stack.

My Vision

My vision is to learn as much as I can in the industry, school, and on my own during the next couple years so that I can make an informed decision about where I see myself in the future. As of now, there are so many aspects of technology that inspire me. I find it difficult to choose one linear path without knowing more about the different possibilities at hand. I want to become a better software engineer, data scientist, web developer, statistician, mathematician, software manager, and so on. Depending on what I end up liking the best, I may end focusing on either software or data. I may also deicide to integrate the two.

I do know one thing for sure - I would love to be on the forefront of new technological innovations and break-throughs. That was my primary reason for seeking higher education. As much as I could have succeeded in the field without graduate school, I believe that it will allow me to have a higher level understanding that cannot be gained from a three month program. During my undergaduate career at the University of Minnesota, I gained a B.A. in Physiology with minors in Public Health and Arabic. Although I enjoyed my studies, they have no relation to computer science, propelling me to pursue a technical degree at the University of Saint Thomas.
